# Safety & Misuse Boundaries — Model 1 v1.0

## Safety position

Model 1 v1.0 is a **first-person-only stop-loss guardrail**. It is designed to help a person detect whether their own next step requires slowing down, freezing, or exiting.

It is not designed to analyze or control other people.

## Mandatory boundaries

### 1. First-person only

Permitted use concerns the user's own:

- boundary,
- cost exposure,
- reversibility,
- proposed next step,
- decision to pause, freeze, or exit.

Not permitted:

- diagnosing, labelling, or profiling another person,
- mapping another person's weaknesses or likely reactions,
- deciding what another person “really is,”
- producing tactics to gain compliance or emotional leverage.

### 2. No weaponization

This repository must not be used for:

- coercion,
- manipulation,
- persuasion optimization,
- pressure sequencing,
- exploitation of vulnerability,
- harassment or retaliation.

A framework intended to detect whether **I should stop** must not be converted into a system for deciding **how to move someone else**.

### 3. No automated authority

A Green, Yellow, or Red signal does not authorize a real-world action.

The protocol must not be wired into a system that automatically:

- sends messages,
- changes access or permissions,
- spends money,
- commits code or publishes content,
- makes legal, medical, employment, or safety decisions,
- triggers actions against another person.

### 4. Not a professional substitute

This repository is not therapy, counseling, medical advice, legal advice, emergency response, or regulatory approval.

Where professional or emergency systems are required, the protocol may at most help the user pause and organize their own questions. It does not replace qualified professionals or emergency services.

### 5. Privacy and public-data boundary

Do not put identifiable private medical, financial, employment, relationship, or safety information into public tools or public repository material.

Public examples, if any are later released, must be de-identified and reduced to reusable structure only.

## Invalid uses

Any output is invalid if it:

- makes claims about another person's diagnosis, motives, or value,
- tells the user how to control or pressure another person,
- treats AI output as final judgment,
- encourages irreversible action without human review,
- expands the protocol into an all-purpose life system,
- exposes private narrative material for illustration.

## Hard rule

> Keep what helps a person identify whether they should stop.  
> Remove what helps a person influence, push, rank, or control others.
