# Start Here

This is the beginner entry point for **Model 1 v1.0**.

Model 1 is a public-safe judgment support scaffold. It helps a person pause before taking a consequential next step, especially when the situation involves uncertainty, rising cost, unclear sources, or reduced reversibility.

It is not an autonomous system.  
It is not a private engine.  
It is not personal advice.  
It is not a replacement for human judgment.

The purpose of this guide is simple:

> Slow down before action.  
> Check the source.  
> Check the boundary.  
> Keep the final decision human.

---

## 1. What Model 1 is

Model 1 v1.0 is a minimal stop-loss guardrail for judgment.

It helps with questions like:

- Should I pause before acting?
- Is the next step reversible?
- Do I have enough verified information?
- Am I crossing a boundary?
- Is the cost rising faster than the clarity?
- Am I asking an AI system to decide something that I should decide myself?

Model 1 does not give a command.  
It gives a signal.

The final decision stays with the human user.

---

## 2. What Model 1 is not

Model 1 is not:

- a prediction tool for other people;
- a profiling system;
- a persuasion or manipulation guide;
- an automated decision engine;
- therapy, counseling, medical advice, legal advice, financial advice, or investment advice;
- a replacement for professional judgment;
- a system for outsourcing responsibility to AI.

It should not be used to label, pressure, diagnose, rank, or control another person.

It should not be used to justify harmful action.

---

## 3. The basic idea

Before taking a consequential action, ask three simple questions:

1. **What is the next action?**  
   Write the action in plain language.

2. **What could go wrong if I continue?**  
   Look for cost, risk, boundary crossing, missing evidence, or loss of reversibility.

3. **Can I still stop or roll back?**  
   If the answer is unclear, slow down before continuing.

The goal is not to find a perfect answer.

The goal is to prevent avoidable escalation.

---

## 4. The three signals

Model 1 uses three public signals.

### 🟢 Green

No stop-loss trigger is currently visible from the stated input.

This does not mean the action is guaranteed to be correct.  
It only means no major pause signal has been identified yet.

### 🟡 Yellow

Risk, ambiguity, cost, or uncertainty is rising.

Slow down.  
Check sources.  
Clarify the boundary.  
Preserve options.

### 🔴 Red

A hard boundary, irreversible cost, or serious uncertainty is present.

Freezing or exiting may be reasonable.  
Do not treat AI output as permission to continue.  
The human user must make the final decision.

---

## 5. Human Final Gate

Model 1 depends on the **Human Final Gate**.

This means:

- AI may help organize information;
- AI may help identify possible risk signals;
- AI may help ask better questions;
- AI may help slow down the process;

but AI must not become the final decision-maker.

The human user keeps responsibility for the final action.

A useful rule:

> If the action has real-world consequences, the final decision cannot be outsourced to AI.

---

## 6. Source discipline

Model 1 works better when the input is grounded.

Before acting, check:

- What do I actually know?
- What is assumed?
- What has been verified from a real source?
- What is still unclear?
- What would change my decision?

Do not treat a confident explanation as verified evidence.

A clear source is stronger than a convincing story.

---

## 7. Public/private boundary

This public repository only contains the beginner-safe scaffold.

It may include:

- beginner guides;
- public-safe definitions;
- simple examples;
- boundary notes;
- decision gates;
- signal vocabulary.

It does not include:

- private cases;
- identifiable personal material;
- private calibration chains;
- sensitive judgment records;
- relationship, family, medical, workplace, or financial details;
- complete private engine logic;
- hidden runtime packs or executable decision systems.

The public layer is intentionally limited.

That limit is part of the safety design.

---

## 8. A simple beginner workflow

Use this minimal workflow:

1. Write the next action.
2. Check whether the action has real-world consequences.
3. Check whether key facts have been verified from real sources.
4. Check whether the action is still reversible.
5. Mark the signal:
   - 🟢 Green
   - 🟡 Yellow
   - 🔴 Red
6. Apply the Human Final Gate.
7. Decide whether to continue, slow down, freeze, or exit.

The signal is not a command.

It is a pause-and-check tool.

---

## 9. Public-safe example

Suppose you are about to send an important email.

Before sending, you can ask:

- Is this email reversible?
- Did I verify the key facts?
- Could this create cost or conflict?
- Am I reacting too quickly?
- Would waiting improve the decision?

Possible signal:

- 🟢 Green: the facts are checked and the email is low-risk.
- 🟡 Yellow: some facts are unclear, or the tone may create avoidable problems.
- 🔴 Red: the email could create serious consequences and cannot easily be undone.

The final decision remains with the sender.
